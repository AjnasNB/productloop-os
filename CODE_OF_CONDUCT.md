# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

ProductLoop OS contributors, reviewers, and maintainers commit to making participation respectful and free from harassment, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity or expression, experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, appearance, race, caste, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We will act in ways that support an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Expected behavior

Examples of positive behavior include:

- showing empathy and respect for different viewpoints and experience levels;
- giving specific, constructive technical feedback;
- accepting responsibility, apologizing when appropriate, and repairing harm;
- keeping disagreement focused on code, evidence, risk, and project goals; and
- respecting privacy, security boundaries, maintainer decisions, and contributor attribution.

## Unacceptable behavior

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- sexualized language, imagery, or unwanted attention;
- trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, threats, or personal attacks;
- public or private harassment;
- publishing another person's private information without explicit permission;
- pressuring contributors to disclose credentials, private data, or employer information;
- impersonating the project or claiming ungranted merge, release, or representation authority; and
- other conduct that would reasonably be inappropriate in a professional community.

## Scope

This policy applies in repository issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, release coordination, and other public or private spaces where someone represents ProductLoop OS.

## Enforcement

Project maintainer `@AjnasNB` is responsible for interpreting and enforcing this policy and may edit or remove contributions, comments, commits, code, issues, or other material that violates it. Responses may include a correction request, warning, temporary restriction, or permanent exclusion, depending on context and impact.

Report conduct concerns to `@AjnasNB` through GitHub. Do not post sensitive details publicly. When a report requires a private channel, use the repository's private reporting route described in [SECURITY.md](./SECURITY.md) and identify the submission as a code-of-conduct report. Reports will be reviewed with respect for the privacy and safety of the reporter and affected people.

Retaliation against someone who raises a good-faith concern is not permitted. The maintainer will disclose information only as reasonably necessary to review and respond to the report.
